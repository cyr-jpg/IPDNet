# IPDNet
Abstract—Detecting extremely small objects in large-scale remote sensing imagery remains challenging due to progressive information degradation caused by repeated downsampling and feature transformation. The loss of high-frequency structural details significantly weakens discriminative representations, leading to unstable localization and limited robustness under complex backgrounds. To address this issue, we propose an Information-Preserving Detection Network (IPDNet) under an information-fidelity–driven design paradigm. Specifically, a Frequency-Aware Downsampling (FAD) module based on discrete wavelet transform is introduced to explicitly decouple and enhance high-frequency structural components, mitigating spectral aliasing during scale compression. A Re-parameterizable Asymmetric Convolution module (RepASConv) is further designed to strengthen directional structure modeling during training while maintaining inference efficiency via branch fusion. To improve robustness against background interference, a Hybrid Context Attention (HCA) mechanism jointly models multi-statistic channel dependencies and multi-scale spatial context through adaptive weighting. Moreover, a Shape-aware Normalized Wasserstein Distance (Shape-NWD) loss is developed to stabilize regression gradients for extremely small objects by incorporating distribution-aware and shape-constrained optimization. A high-resolution multi-scale strategy is additionally employed to suppress structural collapse induced by excessive downsampling. Extensive experiments on AI-TOD, VisDrone2019, and VEDAI demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed framework. On the AI-TOD dataset, IPDNet improves mAP@50 by 11%–14.8% and mAP@50-95 by 4.6%–8.3%, while further reducing parameter complexity. These results validate that the proposed method achieves a favorable balance among accuracy, robustness, and computational efficiency for extremely small object detection in remote sensing imagery.
